WEATHER TYPES:

Explore weather types & forecasts!

Cloudy

Overcast skies and cooler weather, ideal for light farm work.

Sunny

Clear skies and ideal conditions for drying crops or fieldwork.

Rainy

Wet conditions ahead; protect sensitive crops and manage drainage.

Stormy

Severe weather; secure tools and pause field activities.

PEST CONTROL:

Learn about common pests and how to protect your farm!

Aphids

Tiny, sap-sucking insects that can stunt plant growth and transmit viruses.

How to Deal:
  • Use neem oil or insecticidal soap.
  • Introduce ladybugs (natural predators).
  • Hose off plants with strong water spray.
Warnings:

While generally harmless to humans, large infestations can devastate crops quickly by weakening plants and spreading diseases.

Armyworms

Larvae that feed on leaves and stems, causing significant defoliation.

How to Deal:
  • Apply Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) spray.
  • Hand-pick and destroy visible larvae.
  • Maintain good field sanitation.
Warnings:

Armyworms can cause widespread crop damage in a short period, leading to substantial yield losses. Not directly harmful to humans.

Rodents (Rats/Mice)

Can damage crops by gnawing on plants, consuming stored grains, and contaminating food.

How to Deal:
  • Set traps (snap traps, live traps).
  • Eliminate food sources and secure storage areas.
  • Encourage natural predators (e.g., owls).
Warnings:

Can transmit diseases like leptospirosis, hantavirus, and salmonellosis through their droppings and urine.

Locusts/Grasshoppers

Highly migratory pests that can consume entire fields of crops in a very short time.

How to Deal:
  • Early detection and targeted insecticide application.
  • Biological control (fungal biopesticides).
  • Community-wide monitoring and control efforts.
Warnings:

Locust swarms are one of the most destructive agricultural pests, capable of causing widespread famine by destroying all vegetation. Not directly harmful to humans but can cause severe food insecurity.
